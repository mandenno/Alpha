  • Who is a health donor?

  • Any one whose blood is devoid of HIV/AIDs, hepatitis B, hepatitis C and Syphilis.

  • Who is eligible to donate?

  • Usually, any person within the age group of 18-60 years within a minimum body weight of 45Kgs and having a minimum haemoglobin content of 12g/dL is eligible to donate. All countries have criteria for blood donation in their respective countries. In Kenya, one has to be 16 to 65 years of age, above 50Kgs with a safe sexual lifestyle.

  • How much blood do I donate?

  • 1 pint (450 ml)

  • How long does it take for my body to replenish the blood I have donated?

  • Fluid part (plasma) is replaced within 24 hrs, while the cells part is replaced within two weeks.

  • What are the benefits of donating blood?

  • Saving lives, knowing your blood group, knowing your status, altruism, patriotism.

  • After how long should I donate?

  • After every 3 months for males and after 4 months for females.
